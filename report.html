Trader Behavior & Market Sentiment Analysis

📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes historical trader data alongside the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index to understand how trading behavior (profitability, risk, volume, leverage) aligns or diverges from overall market sentiment. The analysis aims to identify patterns that could guide smarter trading strategies.

Core Question: How does trader performance vary between periods of market Fear and Greed?

📂 Dataset Description

  • Historical Trader Data: Account, symbol, execution price, size, side, timestamp, position, closed PnL, leverage, fees
  • Fear & Greed Index: Daily classification (Fear, Extreme Fear, Greed, Extreme Greed) with sentiment score

🔍 Key Findings

  • Average Closed PnL is significantly higher on Greed days compared to Fear days.
  • Leverage usage tends to increase during Greed periods, suggesting more aggressive trading behavior.
  • Trade volumes spike during sentiment extremes, but not all volume translates into profit.

📉 Performance by Sentiment

  • Fear Days: Lower PnL, more cautious trades, smaller average position sizes.
  • Greed Days: Higher PnL potential but also increased risk from higher leverage.

🛠 Project Workflow

  1. Loaded trader and sentiment datasets
  2. Cleaned data, standardized date formats
  3. Merged datasets on date for combined analysis
  4. Calculated metrics: average PnL, leverage, volume per sentiment
  5. Visualized results using bar charts and line plots

📊 Key Visualizations

  • Average Closed PnL by Sentiment – Bar Chart
  • Leverage Trends by Sentiment – Line Chart
  • Trade Volume Distribution – Histogram

💡 Recommendations

  • During Greed periods, apply stricter risk management to avoid losses from over-leveraging.
  • During Fear periods, explore contrarian strategies for potential gains.
  • Monitor sentiment shifts as early indicators for changing trade setups.
